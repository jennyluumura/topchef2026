📺 Episode summaries
Ep 1 Charlotte Motor Speedway Mar 9, 2026
The season opened at Charlotte Motor Speedway with NASCAR legends Kyle Busch and Jimmie Johnson as guest judges. Chefs competed in teams for the Quickfire, then drew knives for sweet potato varietals in the elimination challenge. Day Joseph's Carolina Ruby sweet potato dish suffered from undercooked fish and overpowering herbs, making her the first chef eliminated — and without a shot at Last Chance Kitchen.
Ep 2 PuckerButt Mar 16, 2026
Guest judge Mei Lin joined for a fiery episode built around Carolina Reapers. Laurence Louie took the Quickfire. In the elimination, chefs created a seven-course progressively spicier dinner — Rhoda Magbitang won best dish with her pepper-braised short rib, while Sieger Bayer and Jennifer Lee Jackson landed in the top group. Nana Araba Wilmot and Justin Tootla fell to the bottom group. Jassi Bindra's lamb vindaloo wasn't spicy enough and came out overcooked, sending him home eliminated — also without a Last Chance Kitchen lifeline.
Ep 3 Color & Livermush Mar 23, 2026
Emeril Lagasse surprised the chefs as guest judge. Anthony Jones — a North Carolina local who knows his livermush — won the Quickfire. The elimination challenge asked chefs to incorporate two naturally-dyed elements as a nod to NC's textile industry. Laurence Louie claimed the winning dish, with Anthony and Brandon Dearden rounding out the top group. Every other competing chef landed in the bottom group (−1 each) in a tough judging week. Nana Araba Wilmot was eliminated after failing to complete her dish with the required color elements.
Ep 4 Pick a Side Mar 30, 2026
Season 22 winner Tristen Epps-Long returned as guest judge. The Quickfire had chefs create wine-paired food flights in teams of three — the Blue Team of Sieger Bayer, Justin Tootla, and Jennifer Lee Jackson won (+1 each). In the elimination, chefs visited Deep Roots Farm then turned classic Southern side dishes into main courses. Sieger took a risk on fried okra that paid off, claiming the winning dish (+2). Laurence Louie and Sherry Cardoso joined him in the top group, while Anthony Jones and Jennifer fell to the bottom. Brittany Cochran's collard greens never steamed — leaving her with raw pork and no time to recover — and she was eliminated.
Ep 5 Cut and Dry Apr 6, 2026
Comedian Fortune Feimster guest judged an improv Quickfire alongside three kids, with parameters of "Christmas, Colorado, and Puffified." Brandon Dearden's Colorado vanilla ice cream with corn nut crunch won the challenge (+1). Jennifer Lee Jackson sat out the Quickfire due to a shoulder injury but was warned she cannot miss another. The elimination took chefs to the U.S. National Whitewater Center to cook dishes featuring dehydrated components. Anthony Jones' salmon was flawless, earning the winning dish and immunity (+2), with Sherry Cardoso and Duyen Ha in the top group. Laurence Louie and Oscar Diaz landed in the bottom. Rhoda Magbitang's monkfish arrived raw and leeching water — a stunning fall for the chef who won the first two eliminations — and she was eliminated.
Ep 6 Going Whole Hog Apr 13, 2026
No Quickfire this week — chefs went straight into an all-night whole hog barbecue elimination. Split into two teams of five, the Red Team (Anthony, Laurence, Brandon, Sherry, Duyen) faced off against the Gray Team (Sieger, Jonathan, Oscar, Justin, Jennifer) across five head-to-head rounds judged by local pitmasters. The Red Team swept the first three rounds: Sherry Cardoso won Round 1 (shoulder) and Brandon Dearden won Round 3 (belly), both landing in the top group (+1 each). Laurence Louie won Round 2 (loin) and was named the overall winning dish (+2). On the Gray Team, Oscar Diaz earned +1 for winning the s'mores challenge, while Justin Tootla and Jonathan Dearden lost their rounds and landed in the bottom (−1 each). Sieger Bayer was eliminated after his pork loin came out dry — a tough exit for the Ep 4 winner who refused to stray from traditional whole hog technique.
Ep 7 Episode 7 Apr 20, 2026
The Quickfire was a team challenge won by Anthony Jones, Brandon Dearden, and Jonathan Dearden (+1 each). In the elimination challenge, Anthony Jones delivered his second winning dish of the season (+2, for a Ep 7 total of +3). Laurence Louie, Sherry Cardoso, and Brandon Dearden rounded out the top group (+1 each, Brandon's Ep 7 total +2). Duyen Ha, Oscar Diaz, and Jonathan Dearden fell to the bottom (−1 each, Jonathan's Ep 7 total 0). Justin Tootla was eliminated (−2) after struggling to keep pace with the competition.
Ep 8 Medical Emergency Apr 27, 2026
There was no Quickfire this week due to Jennifer Lee Jackson withdrawing from the competition for medical reasons — she was not formally eliminated but is no longer competing. Sieger Bayer returned to the competition after his Last Chance Kitchen run. In the elimination, Duyen Ha finally broke through with the winning dish (+2), with Laurence Louie, Sherry Cardoso, and Anthony Jones in the top group (+1 each). Sieger Bayer, Jonathan Dearden, and Oscar Diaz landed in the bottom (−1 each). Brandon Dearden was eliminated (−2) for his disappointing dish, ending his impressive run in the competition.
